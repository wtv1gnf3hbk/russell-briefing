STYLE RULES (loaded from style-rules-prompt.txt — do not edit inline):
1. NEVER use the word "amid." Use "as", "during", "while", or "following" instead.
2. Link text must be MAX 3 WORDS. GOOD: "Ukraine [rejected the](url) ceasefire" — BAD: "[Ukraine rejects latest Russian ceasefire proposal](url)"
3. NEVER use 's as a contraction for "is" or "has" — only use 's for possessives. BAD: "China's planning" → GOOD: "China is planning". OK: "China's economy" (possessive).
4. NEVER use em-dashes to join independent clauses. Write separate sentences instead.
5. NEVER use meta-news framing — don't say "The biggest story today is..." or "...is drawing global attention" or "...is leading [outlet]." Just report the news.
6. NO editorializing. No "saber-rattling," "reaching a crescendo," "makes diplomats nervous," "brutal blow." Report facts, not vibes.
7. NO tacked-on analysis. End bullets with the fact, not the implication. Delete: ", showing how...", ", highlighting...", ", underscoring...", ", suggesting that...", ", a testament to...", "It's a reminder...", "This signals..."
8. Do NOT use ", while" to connect two unrelated news items. Separate stories get separate sentences. "While" is fine for genuinely related clauses or meaning "during."
9. When linking to a non-NYT source, ALWAYS attribute it: "Reuters reports", "according to the BBC", "per the FT." The link alone is not enough.
10. Be specific with numbers: "fell 8% to $68,000" not "stumbling" or "volatile." Precision over drama.
11. ONLY use URLs that appear in the story data. NEVER fabricate, guess, or construct URLs from headlines.
